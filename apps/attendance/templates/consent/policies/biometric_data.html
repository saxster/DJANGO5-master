Biometric Data Collection and Use Consent

Important Notice: This consent form is required for collection of your biometric information (face recognition templates) in compliance with applicable state biometric privacy laws.

What is Biometric Data?

Biometric data refers to unique physical characteristics used for identification. In our system, this includes:

  • Facial recognition templates (mathematical representations of your facial features)
  • Face images captured during clock-in/out

Important: We store only mathematical templates, NOT actual photographs of your face.

Purpose of Collection

We collect your biometric data for these specific purposes:

  • Verify your identity during clock-in and clock-out
  • Prevent time theft and "buddy punching"
  • Ensure accurate payroll processing
  • Maintain security of our facilities and client sites

How We Protect Your Biometric Data

  • Encryption: All biometric templates are encrypted using industry-standard AES-256 encryption
  • Access Controls: Only authorized systems can access biometric data
  • No Sale or Profit: We will NEVER sell or profit from your biometric information
  • No Third-Party Sharing: Biometric data is not shared with third parties except as required by law
  • Secure Storage: Data stored in secure, encrypted databases

Data Retention and Destruction

  • During Employment: Retained while you are employed
  • After Employment: Deleted within 30 days of termination or resignation
  • Upon Revocation: Deleted within 30 days if you revoke consent
  • Destruction Method: Permanent deletion (unrecoverable)

Your Rights

You have the right to:

  • Receive a copy of this written policy
  • Receive a publicly available retention schedule and destruction guidelines
  • Revoke consent at any time in writing
  • Request deletion of your biometric data
  • Use alternative identification methods (where available)
  • File a complaint if you believe your biometric data has been misused

Alternative Methods

If you choose not to provide consent for biometric data collection, alternative attendance verification methods may be available:

  • Supervisor verification of attendance
  • Physical sign-in sheets (where applicable)
  • QR code scanning at work sites

Note: Some positions may require biometric verification as an essential job function for security compliance.

State-Specific Compliance

This policy complies with:

  • Illinois Biometric Information Privacy Act (BIPA)
  • Texas Capture or Use of Biometric Identifier Act (CUBI)
  • Washington State Biometric Privacy Law (HB 1493)
  • California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) biometric provisions
