Written Consent for GPS Location Tracking

State of Louisiana - LA Rev Stat ยง 14:323 Compliance

Louisiana Law Requirement: Louisiana Revised Statutes 14:323 requires written consent before tracking a person's location via GPS.

Consent Statement

I, {{ employee.first_name }} {{ employee.last_name }} (Employee ID: {{ employee.id }}), hereby provide my explicit written consent to my employer for GPS location tracking during my work hours for the following purposes:

  • Verification of attendance at assigned work locations
  • Employee safety and emergency response
  • Calculation of mileage for expense reimbursement
  • Compliance with client site access requirements

Terms of Consent

  1. Scope: GPS tracking will occur ONLY during scheduled work hours when I am on duty
  2. Devices: Tracking applies to company-provided devices and my personal device if I choose to use it for work clock-in/out
  3. Data Use: Location data will be used solely for legitimate business purposes as described above
  4. Data Retention: GPS data will be retained for 90 days, then archived for 2 years for payroll purposes
  5. Access: Only authorized personnel (supervisors, HR, payroll, security) may access my location data
  6. Revocation: I may revoke this consent at any time by providing written notice to HR
