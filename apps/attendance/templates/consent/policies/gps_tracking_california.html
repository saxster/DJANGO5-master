GPS Location Tracking Consent Policy

Jurisdiction: State of California

Version: 1.0

Effective Date: November 1, 2025

Plain Language Summary: This policy explains how we track your location during work hours using GPS technology, and seeks your explicit consent as required by California law.

1. Purpose of GPS Tracking

We use GPS location tracking for the following legitimate business purposes:

  • Verify attendance at assigned work locations
  • Ensure employee safety and security
  • Optimize routing and scheduling
  • Comply with client requirements for site access verification
  • Calculate mileage for expense reimbursement

2. What Data We Collect

When you clock in or out, we collect:

  • GPS coordinates (latitude and longitude)
  • Timestamp of when location was captured
  • GPS accuracy (in meters)
  • Device identifier
  • Whether location is within assigned geofence

3. When We Track Your Location

Work Hours Only: GPS tracking is ONLY active during your scheduled work hours when you:

  • Clock in at the start of your shift
  • Clock out at the end of your shift
  • Take breaks (if required by your assignment)
California Law Protection: We do NOT track your location during non-work hours, on personal time, or when you are off-duty. Tracking is limited to work-related activities only.

4. Company vs. Personal Devices

  • Company-Provided Devices: May be tracked during work hours
  • Personal Devices: Tracking requires your explicit consent and you may use alternative attendance methods if you prefer not to be tracked on your personal device

5. How We Use Your Location Data

Your GPS data is used to:

  • Validate you are at the correct work site
  • Generate attendance records for payroll
  • Calculate travel distance for reimbursement
  • Respond to safety emergencies
  • Investigate time and attendance discrepancies

6. Who Can Access Your Location Data

  • Your direct supervisor/manager
  • HR and payroll personnel
  • System administrators (for technical support only)
  • Authorized security personnel (emergency situations)

All access is logged and audited for compliance.

7. Data Retention

  • Active Period: 90 days of detailed GPS history
  • Archived Period: 2 years (summary data only for payroll reconciliation)
  • After Employment: GPS data deleted within 30 days of termination

8. Your Rights Under California Law

You have the right to:

  • Revoke Consent: You may withdraw consent at any time
  • Access Your Data: Request a copy of your location history
  • Correct Inaccuracies: Request correction of incorrect location data
  • Delete Your Data: Request deletion (subject to legal retention requirements)
  • Alternative Methods: Use non-GPS attendance methods

9. Data Security

We protect your GPS location data using:

  • Encryption at rest and in transit (AES-256)
  • Access controls and authentication
  • Comprehensive audit logging
  • Regular security audits
  • Compliance with industry best practices

10. Consent is Voluntary

Your consent is voluntary. If you choose not to provide consent:

  • You will not be retaliated against
  • Alternative attendance methods will be provided
  • Your employment status will not be affected
Note: Some positions may require GPS tracking as a essential job function (e.g., mobile field workers). This will be clearly stated in your job description.

11. Changes to This Policy

If we make material changes to this policy, we will:

  • Notify you via email at least 30 days in advance
  • Request renewed consent if changes are substantial
  • Provide opportunity to revoke consent before changes take effect
